<div><h3>Vijay Kumar P. Mehra v Premlata Mehra and Others
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>15 January 2004
</b><p>
</p><p>C.A. No. 1977 of 1998
</p><p>The Order of the Court was as follows:
</p><p>Heard learned counsel for the appellant.
</p><p>1.  In substance the appellant seeks to fix liability on the plaintiff-respondents and make them liable for the loss occasioned to the appellant on account of a bona fide mistake committed by the receiver who is an officer of the Court.
</p><p>2. Though loss may have been caused to the appellant, the plaintiff-respondents cannot be burdened with the liability. We do not find any cause to interfere with the view taken by the High Court. The appeal is dismissed.
</p><p>Appeal dismissed.
</p></div>
