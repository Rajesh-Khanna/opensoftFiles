<div><h3>Darbara Singh v Meeto and Others
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>23 February 2001
</b><p>
</p><p>Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.12102/2000
</p><p>The order of the court was as follows:
</p><p>Heard learned counsel for the parties.
</p><p>Leave granted.
</p><p>1.  Pursuant to the order dated 15th December, 2000 passed by this Court the appellant has deposited the decretal amount before the Executing Court. This Court had also directed the Executing Court to pay the said amount to the judgment creditor after due verification.
</p><p>2. Considering the facts and circumstances, in our view, the order passed by the Additional District Judge, Patiala, directing the appellant to pay interest 12% P.A., on the damages awarded, from the date of the suit till the recovery is set aside. Rest of the order passed by the Additional District Judge granting damages would remain undisturbed and it is stated in the Court that the same has already been implemented. The appeal is disposed of in above terms. No costs.
</p><p>Appeal disposed of.
</p></div>
