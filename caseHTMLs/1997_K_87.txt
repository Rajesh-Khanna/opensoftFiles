<div><h3>Collector of Central Excise, Chandigarh v Kaushal Steel Rolling Mills and Others
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>15 July 1997
</b><p>
</p><p>C.As. No. 107(NM) of 1988 with Nos. 1647-70(NM) of 1987, 2722-2756 and 2159-2192 of 1996
</p><p>The Order of the Court was as follows:
</p><p>1. The learned Additional Solicitor General states that these matters relate to classification of "bars", "flats", "hop", "strips" manufactured by the respondents. They are covered by the judgment of this Court in CCE v. Tigrania Metal and Steel Industries <a href="/case/<a href="/case/1996 Indlaw SC 212">1996 Indlaw SC 212</a>0"><a href="/case/1996 Indlaw SC 212">1996 Indlaw SC 212</a>0</a> wherein it has been held that they fall under Tariff Item 26-AA, sub-item (i-a) of the erstwhile Central Excise Tariff.
</p><p>2. The appeals are, therefore, dismissed with no order as to costs.
</p><p>Appeals dismissed.
