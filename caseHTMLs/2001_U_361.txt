<div><h3>T.V. Venogopal v Ushodaya Enterprises Limited and Anr
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>10 September 2001
</b><p>
</p><p>Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.11055-11056/2001
</p><p>The Order of the Court was as follows:
</p><p>1.  I.As. for exemption from filing retyped documents and permission to place additional documents on record are allowed.
</p><p>Special leave granted.
</p><p>2.  Pending hearing of the appeals, the appellant is permitted to sell agarbattis outside the State of Andhra Pradesh by using the expression EENADU in the languages of the respective States and in English but he shall not use the script of the word EENADU in any manner in Telugu.
</p><p>3. The appellant is, however, injuncted from using the expression EENADU within the geographical limits of the State of Andhra Pradesh, in any language.
</p><p>Petition allowed.
</p></div>
