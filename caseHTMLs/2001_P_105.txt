<div><h3>Pedidam Chenchu Munuswamy and Anr v Pedidam China Chenchu Munuswamy and Others
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>23 March 2001
</b><p>
</p><p>Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.8082/2000
</p><p>The Order of the Court was as follows:
</p><p>1.  This matter arises out of interlocutory order made by the High court. However, when this matter came up before this Court by way of a special leave petition, while issuing notice, the order made by the High Court was stayed. It is stated that, however, pursuant to the order made by the High Court, the amount has been deposited in the Reference Court.
</p><p>2.  It is unnecessary for us to examine the merits of the case.
</p><p>3.  It would be appropriate for the parties to urge their respective cases before the High Court and the interim order granted by this Court will enure to the benefits of the parties during the pendency of the matter before the High Court. The special leave petition is disposed of accordingly.
</p><p>Petition disposed of.
</p></div>
