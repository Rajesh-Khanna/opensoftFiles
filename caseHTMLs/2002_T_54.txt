<div><h3>Thapar Oil & Fats Ltd v Rajasthan S.I.D.&Investment Corp.Ltd&Ors
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>30 September 2002
</b><p>
</p><p>Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.1777/2002
</p><p>The Order of the Court was as follows:
</p><p>1.  The petitioner came before this Court on the view that asagainst the order of the learned Single Judge no letters patent appeal was available and therefore he could come by way of this special leave petition for leave to appeal under Article 136 of the Constitution of India.
</p><p>2.  Heard the learned counsel on the either side. The learned senior counsel appearing for the petitioner represents that now, since the question as to whether the letters patent appeal against the order of the learned Single Judge lies or not is pending consideration before a larger Bench of the Rajasthan High Court and appeals have been also entertained by the High Court for consideration he, therefore, seeks permission to withdraw this petition to pursue his remedy before the High Court.
</p><p>3.  In the light of the above request made permission is granted to the petitioner to withdraw this petition, to enable him to pursue his remedies before the High Court itself as noticed above.
</p><p>Petition disposed of.
</p></div>
