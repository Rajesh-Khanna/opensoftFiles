<div><h3>Commissioner of Income Tax v Cibatul Limited
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>16 December 1997
</b><p>
</p><p>C.A. No. 3310 of 1993 (Arising as an appeal from the judgment and order Dt. 26 April 1988, of the Gujarat High Court in I.T.R. No. 98 of 1987).
</p><p>The Order of the Court was as follows:
</p><p>The following question of law was referred to the High Court:
</p><p>"Whether, on the facts and in the circumstances of the case and in law, the cost of uninstalled machinery of Rs. 14, 71, 833 is to be taken into account in the computation of the capital for computing the relief under section 80J of the Act?"
</p><p>1.  The High Court answered the question in favour of the assessee relying on the decision in the case of CIT v. Cibatul Ltd. 1978 Indlaw GUJ 27. This judgment has been approved by this court in the case of CIT v. Alcock Ashdown and Co. Ltd. <a href="/case/1997 Indlaw SC 2533">1997 Indlaw SC 2533</a>.
</p><p>2. In view of the aforesaid, the appeal is dismissed. There will be no order as to costs.
</p><p>Appeal dismissed.
</p></div>
