<div><h3>Babulal and another v Municipal Corporation, Ratlam and others
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>15 July 2005
</b><p>
</p><p>C.A. No. 3765 of 2005 (Arising out of S.L.P.(C) No. 5743 of 2004)
</p><p>The Order of the Court was as follows:
</p><p>Leave granted.
</p><p>1.  The High Court appears to have proceeded on the basis that it was settled law that a person not a party to the original proceedings cannot be proceeded against in contempt. The issue is a debatable one, particularly having regard to the law on the issue particularly in S.N. Banerjee v. Kuchwar Lime and Stone Co. Ltd. 1938 Indlaw PC 54.
</p><p>2. The order of the High Court is set aside and the matter is remanded back for reconsideration of the issue.
</p><p>The appeal is disposed of accordingly.
</p><p>Appeal disposed of.
</p></div>
