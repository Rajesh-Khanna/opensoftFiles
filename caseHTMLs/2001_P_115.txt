<div><h3>Piara Singh and Others v Kundan Singh
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>10 August 2001
</b><p>
</p><p>Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.14553/2000
</p><p>The Order of the Court was as follows:
</p><p>1.  Learned counsel for the petitioners submit that the question of jurisdiction of the civil court for entertaining this suit has infact been raised before the High Court and the High Court has not considered the same. Learned counsel further submits that petitioners would be advised to file an application for review on that ground before the High Court. They would also be advised to file an application for condonation of delay in filing the review petition on the premise that SLP was pending in the meanwhile.
</p><p>2. For adopting the said course of action he seeks for permission of this Court to withdraw this SLP. It is accordingly dismissed as withdrawn.
</p><p>Petition dismissed.
</p></div>
