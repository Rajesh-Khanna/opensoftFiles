<div><h3>Suptd. of Police and Others v M.G. Ramakrishnegowda
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>1 March 2001
</b><p>
</p><p>Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.17417/1999
</p><p>The Order of the Court was as follows:
</p><p>Heard learned counsel for the parties.
</p><p>1.  An affidavit by the Deputy Superintendent of Police (Wireless), Office of the Superintendent of Police (Wireless) No.1, M.G.Road, Bangalore has been filed in which it is averred, inter alia, that out of deference to certain observations of this Court the State Government has decided to appoint the respondent M.G.Ramakrishna Gowda as a peon (Dalayath) w.e.f. 1.2.2001 in the pay scale of Rs.2500-3850 subject to the rules. Learned counsel for the respondent states that the petitioner has accepted the offer and joined the post.
</p><p>2. In view of the development in the case as noted above we dispose of this special leave petition in terms of the aforementioned affidavit. It is made clear that the respondent shall not claim any backwage for the period prior to Ist February, 2001.
</p><p>Order accordingly.
</p></div>
