<div><h3>Commissioner of Customs and Central Excise, Meerut v Pawan Kumar Gupta and another
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>29 April 2005
</b><p>
</p><p>S.L.Ps. (C) Nos. 23673-74 of 2003 with Nos. 20311-12 of 2004 and 5870 of 2005
</p><p>The Order of the Court was as follows:
</p><p>1.  Learned counsel for the petitioner seeks permission to delete the name of Respondent 2 from the array of the parties. Prayer granted at the risk of the petitioner.
</p><p>2. Learned counsel for the petitioner further submits that he is prepared to pay penalty imposed on him and subject to the result of this petition the respondent may be directed to release the goods. We find no reason as to why the goods should not be released when the duty and penalty has been paid subject to the result of the petition.
</p><p>Leave granted in all the matters.
</p><p>3.  Printing dispensed with. The appeals shall be heard on the SLP paper-books. Parties may file additional documents, if any, within ten weeks. Original record need not be called for.
</p><p>Order accordingly.
</p></div>
