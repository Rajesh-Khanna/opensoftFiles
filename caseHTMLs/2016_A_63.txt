<div><h3>Delhi Development Authority v Amarjeet Rewari and others
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>31 August 2016
</b><p>The Judgment was delivered by :<i> Kurian Joseph, J.
</i></p><p>
</p><p>Civil Appeal No. 8507 of 2016 (Arising out of SLP (Civil) No. 17716 of 2015)
</p><p>1. Leave granted.
</p><p>2. The issue, in principle, is covered against the appellant by judgment in Civil Appeal No.8477 of 2016 arising out of Special Leave Petition(Civil)No.8467 of 2015.
</p><p>3. This appeal is, accordingly, dismissed.
</p><p>4. In the peculiar facts and circumstances of this case, the appellant is given a period of one year to exercise its liberty granted under Section 24(2) of the <a href="/acts/Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013">Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013</a> for initiation of the acquisition proceedings afresh.
</p><p>5. We make it clear that in case no fresh acquisition proceedings are initiated within the said period of one year from today by issuing a Notification under Section 11 of the Act, the appellant, if in possession, shall return the physical possession of the land to the original land owner.
</p><p>6. Pending applications, if any, stand disposed of.
</p><p>Appeal dismissed
