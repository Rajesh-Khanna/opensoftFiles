<div><h3>National Insurance Co. Ltd. and another v P. Achayamma and others
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>31 January 2003
</b><p>
</p><p>C.A. No. ........... of 2003 (arising out of S.L.P. (C) No. 18196 of 2001)
</p><p>The Order of the Court was as follows:
</p><p>Leave granted.
</p><p>1.  The appellant herein filed an appeal before the National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission against the order passed by the A.P. State Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission. The said appeal was dismissed by the National Commission on the ground that there was delay of 35 days in filing the same. Dismissal of the appellant's appeal by the National Commission is challenged before us.
</p><p>2. We heard the learned counsel on both sides. Having regard to the facts and circumstances of the case, we allow this appeal, however, on payment of costs. The appellant is directed to pay the cost of Rs. 10,000/- to the respondent within a period of three weeks from today. On production of satisfactory proof regarding payment of costs to the respondent before the National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission, the Commission is requested to entertain the appeal filed by the appellant herein and dispose of the same in accordance with law.
</p><p>The appeal stands disposed of accordingly.
</p><p>Appeal disposed of.
