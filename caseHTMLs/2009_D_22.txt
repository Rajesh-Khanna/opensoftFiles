<div><h3>Anand Ramchandra Nerkar & Anr. v Dadar Avanti Coop.Hous.Soc.Ltd.& Ors.
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>6 April 2009
</b><p>
</p><p>Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No(s).7219/2009
</p><p>1. Heard learned counsel for the parties.
</p><p>2. Permission to file the Special Leave Petition is granted.
</p><p>3. No ground is made out for interference u/art. 136 of the Constitution of India. The Special Leave Petitions are therefore, dismissed.
</p><p>4. However, the order of the High Court will not come in the way of flat owners in second floor of an apartment-building from approaching the developer/society for permission to put up an exclusive and separate staircase to his Apartment if such additional access is permissible in accordance with law and if such permission is granted, then renewing the request under Rule 51(iii) of DCR, 1991. This observation shall not be construed as recognition/acceptance of any right to obtain such permission.
</p><p>5. The petitioners are granted six months time to cease the activities of Nursing Home/Clinics subject to the petitioners filing an affidavit within two weeks, undertaking to stop the present activities within six months.
</p><p>Order accordingly
