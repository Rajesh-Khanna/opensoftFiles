<div><h3>General Insurance Corporation of India v Nirmala Popatrao Kale and Others
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>25 January 2001
</b><p>
</p><p>Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.884-885/2001
</p><p>The Order of the Court was as follows:
</p><p>SLP (C) 884-885/2001.
</p><p>1.  The grievance of the petitioner seems to be for the following observations made in the impugned order.
</p><p>"The liabilities due to this payment inter se between Respondents 1, 2 and 3 may be ascertained and settled by Respondents 1, 2 and 3."
</p><p>2.  This is merely recorded for ascertaining settlement inter se between them. We do not find any ground for filing these SLPs. The Special Leave Petitions are dismissed.
</p><p>SLP(C) 1160/2001.
</p><p>The Special Leave Petition is dismissed.
</p><p>Petitions dismissed.
</p></div>
