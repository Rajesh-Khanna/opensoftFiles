<div><h3>Management, State Bank of India v R. Sundaram and Others
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>4 December 2001
</b><p>
</p><p>CIVIL APPEAL NOS. 86-88 OF 1999
</p><p>The Order of the court was as follows:
</p><p>1.  On the special features arising in these cases, the High Court disposed of the same by granting certain limited relief to the respondents.
</p><p>2. In that view of the matter, we do not think we should interfere in exercise of our power under Article 136 of the Constitution.
</p><p>3.  In as much as the High Court disposed of the cases considering the special features arising in these matters, we do not think the same should be applicable to all the matters.
</p><p>The appeals are disposed of accordingly.
</p><p>Appeals disposed of.
</p></div>
