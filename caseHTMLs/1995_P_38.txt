<div><h3>Pamwi Tissues Limited v Collector of Central Excise
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>3 April 1995
</b><p>
</p><p>C.A. No. 3155 of 1993
</p><p>The Order of the Court was as follows:
</p><p>Order Heard on merits.
</p><p>1.  The learned Additional Solicitor General on behalf of the Department submits that the petitioner being a sick unit, there is no objection to hearing of the appeal by the Tribunal without the deposit being made by the appellant, as directed earlier by the Tribunal.
</p><p>2. He submitted that it would be appropriate, in the circumstances of the case that the Tribunal hear the appeal and decide the same on merits without insisting on the deposit of any amount for hearing of the appeal.
</p><p>3.  In view of this statement made on behalf of the respondent, the appeal is allowed. The impugned order of the Tribunal is set aside and the appeal is remitted to the Tribunal for being heard and dated merits.
</p><p>4.  In the circumstances of the case, it would be appropriate that the Tribunal hears and disposes of the appeal within a period of four months from today.
</p><p>Appeal allowed.
</p></div>
