<div><h3>Krishan Gopal v Director, C.B.I., New Delhi
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>7 December 2001
</b><p>
</p><p>Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Cr.) No. 3319/2001
</p><p>The Order of the Court was as follows:
</p><p>1.  During the course of hearing, Mr. Altaf Ahmad, learned Additional Solicitor General produced a letter from the Superintendent of Police, CBI, SCB-I, Delhi, recording therein that CBI has not been involved in the matter in which the notices were issued.
</p><p>2. Let this intimation sent to Shri P. Parmeswaran, Additional Government Advocate, Central Law Agency, Ministry of Law, be kept on the records and in view of such a statement of the learned Additional Solicitor General, the question of any further inquiry in the matter does not arise.
</p><p>As such, the special leave petition stands dismissed.
</p><p>Petition dismissed.
</p></div>
