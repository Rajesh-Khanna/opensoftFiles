<div><h3>Commissioner of Central Excise, Meerut-II v Ester Industries Limited
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>3 January 2001
</b><p>
</p><p>C.A. No. 14 of 2001
</p><p>The Order of the Court was as follows:
</p><p>Issue notice. Mr. Ashok Kumar Singh accepts notice.
</p><p>Leave granted.
</p><p>1.  With the consent of the parties, we have heard the learned counsel on merits. In our opinion, the Tribunal was wrong in declining to make a reference. We, accordingly, direct the Tribunal to refer the following two questions to the High Court:
</p><p>"(a) Whether HSD can be taken as input as mentioned in Section 'AAA' of Central Excise Rules, 1944 and as defined under Rule 57A of Central Excise Rules, 1944 ?
</p><p>(b) Whether the Modvat credit can be allowed on the High Speed Diesel Oil falling under Heading No. 27.10 of <a href="/acts/Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985">Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985</a>, which cannot be taken as inputs and which is specifically excluded from the purview of the Modvat under Rule 57A of Rules ?"
</p><p>The appeal is allowed in the aforesaid terms.
</p><p>Appeal allowed.
</p></div>
