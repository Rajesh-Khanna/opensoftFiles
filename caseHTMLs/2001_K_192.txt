<div><h3>Khurbur v Kallu and Others
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>31 January 2001
</b><p>
</p><p>CIVIL APPEAL NO. 2829 OF 1996
</p><p>The order of the court was as follows:
</p><p>1.  C.A. No. 2829/96: The memo of compromise filed in the Court is taken on record.
</p><p>2.  Mr. Attar Singh and Mr. N.P.Midha, learned counsel for the parties states that the parties to this case have compromised the matter in terms of the compromise memo filed today.
</p><p>3.  In view of the said statement, the appeal is disposed of in terms of the compromise memo referred to above.
</p><p>4.  C.A. No. 2830/96: We do not find any merit in this appeal. It is accordingly dismissed.
</p><p>There shall be no order as to costs.
</p><p>Appeal dismissed.
