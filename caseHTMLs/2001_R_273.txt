<div><h3>Commissioner of Wealth Tax, Meerut v Ravi Shankar
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>27 September 2001
</b><p>
</p><p>C.A. No. 6443 of 2000 - With office report C.A. No. 1582 of 2001 - With office report C.A. No. 1580 of 2001 - With office report
</p><p>The Order of the Court was as follows:
</p><p>1.  The High Court has declined to call for a reference u/s. 27(3) of the Wealth Tax Act. It is found that the findings recorded by the Tribunal are those of fact and no question of law arises from it.
</p><p>2.  Having heard learned counsel for the parties, we find no infirmity in the approach of the High Court.
</p><p>The civil appeals are, accordingly, dismissed.
</p><p>No order as to costs.
</p><p>Appeals dismissed.
</p></div>
