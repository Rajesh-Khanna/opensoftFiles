<div><h3>State of Kerala and Others v K.A. Kumar and Others
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>4  May  2001
</b><p>
</p><p>Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.6928/2001
</p><p>The order of the court was as follows:
</p><p>1.  In view of the fact that the order impugned is interlocutory in nature, we are not inclined to interfere in the matter.
</p><p>2. However, the learned Single Judge while deciding the writ petition shall not be influenced by any finding recorded by the Division Bench in Writ Appeal No.1109/2001.
</p><p>3.  We further direct that promotion, if any, made shall be subject to the decision in the writ petition. The learned Single Judge shall dispose of the petition expeditiously.
</p><p>With these observations, both the special leave petitions are dismissed.
</p><p>Petitions dismissed.
</p></div>
