<div><h3>Atma Ram v Puran Chand Packaging Industries (Private) Limited and others
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>28 February 2005
</b><p>
</p><p>C.A. No. 1441 of 2005 (Arising out of TA No. 1 in & S.L.P. (C) No. 4921 of 2005 (CC No. 2068))
</p><p>The Order of the Court was as follows:
</p><p>Permission to file SLP is granted.
</p><p>Leave granted.
</p><p>1.  Inasmuch as the impugned order passed by the High Court is at one stage of the contempt proceedings which is a matter primarily between the court and the alleged contemnor, we are not inclined to issue notice to the private parties arrayed as respondents.
</p><p>2.  The High Court has by its impugned order dated 9-2-2005 directed the presence of the appellant to be secured by non-bailable warrants.
</p><p>3. It is stated by the learned counsel for the appellant that the appellant has already made appearance in the High Court on 21-2-2005 and the next date appointed is 15-3-2005. He also states that certain directions were made by the High Court which have been complied with by the appellant.
</p><p>4.  Considering the facts and circumstances of the case, that part of the order which directs the presence of the appellant to be secured by non-bailable warrants is set aside. The appellant shall continue to appear in the High Court unless exempted from doing so.
</p><p>The appeal be treated as disposed of.
</p><p>Appeal disposed of.
</p></div>
