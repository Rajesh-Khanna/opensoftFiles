<div><h3>Raparthi Venkata Kankaratnam v Raparthi Pardha Saradhi
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>24 August 2001
</b><p>
</p><p>Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.16099/2000
</p><p>The Order of the Court was as follows:
</p><p>1.  An affidavit has been filed today by the respondent wherein it is stated that the respondent is ready to pay a sum of Rs.1.5 lacs as permanent alimony and also undertaken to spent a sum of Rs.50,000/- at the time of marriage of his daughter Kumari R.V.L.Prasanna.
</p><p>2. This offer is acceptable to the wife, who is present in Court and has been identified by her counsel, Ms. Bina Madhavan. The payment shall be made within three months from today.
</p><p>3.  The respondent shall continue to pay a sum of Rs.1,200/- per month towards the maintenance of the daughter.
</p><p>4.  In view of the said statement, the special leave petition stands disposed of.
</p><p>Petition disposed of.
</p></div>
