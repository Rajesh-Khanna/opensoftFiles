<div><h3>State of Haryana and Others v Ashok Kumar
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>13 December 2001
</b><p>
</p><p>CIVIL APPEAL NO. 2594/2000
</p><p>The Order of the court was as follows:
</p><p>1.  In the facts and circumstances of this case, we do not think it would be appropriate to interfere with the order made by the High Court. This appeal shall stand dismissed.
</p><p>2.  Two points were raised before the High Court that the certificate of eligibility which had been produced by the respondent was not only belated but also that it was a fake one.
</p><p>3. However, here in this Court the fact that allegation that the said certificate is a fake one, is not pursued with.
</p><p>Appeal dismissed.
</p></div>
