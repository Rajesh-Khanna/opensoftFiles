<div><h3>Sukhdev Raj v Union of India and Others
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>4 September 1986
</b><p>
</p><p>C.A. No. 3042 of 1986 in S.L.P. (Civil) No. 1075 of 1986
</p><p>The order of the court was as follows:
</p><p>Special leave granted.
</p><p>1.  Having held that retrenchment was bad and that it should be set aside, the High Court was not justified in not awarding back wages to the petitioner.
</p><p>2.   We direct that the petitioner should be paid back wages within three months from today. The appeal is allowed accordingly. The petitioner is entitled to his costs which we quantify at Rs.500.
</p><p>Appeal allowed.
</p></div>
