<div><h3>Sheo Bachan Pathak v Union of India and Others
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>4 December 2000
</b><p>
</p><p>Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal(Civil)......./2000
</p><p>The order of the court was as follows:
</p><p>Delay condoned.
</p><p>1.  Mr. L.N.Rao appearing for the petitioner states that the High Court committed apparent error by construing the Writ Petition to be one for execution of the order of the Central Administrative Tribunal though in fact the petitioner had approached the High Court for setting aside the order of the Tribunal.
</p><p>2. It would be open for the petitioner to move the High Court for review.
</p><p>3.  We are not inclined to entertain this petition under Article 136 of the Constitution. The Special Leave Petition is dismissed.
</p><p>Petition dismissed.
</p></div>
