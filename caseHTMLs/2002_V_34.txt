<div><h3>Vijay Kumar Batish v State Through Government of N.C.T. of Delhi
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>9 January 2002
</b><p>
</p><p>Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Cr.) No. 7201/2001
</p><p>The Order of the Court was as follows:
</p><p>1.  Though we are not inclined to interfere with the order by which the High Court refused to grant anticipatory bail to the petitioner, we permit him to surrender before the court concerned within two weeks and apply for bail. On such an application the petitioner shall be released on bail on such terms as the court deems fit.
</p><p>2. One of the term should be that the petitioner shall report to the investigating officer for interrogation at a time specified by the court.
</p><p>With these observations the SLP is disposed of.
</p><p>Petition disposed of.
</p></div>
