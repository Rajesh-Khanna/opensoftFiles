<div><h3>Saroj Maheshwari v State of Haryana and others
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>2 September 2016
</b><p>The Judgment was delivered by :<i> Anil R. Dave, J.
</i></p><p>
</p><p>Civil Appeal No(s). 8745-8746 of 2016 (Arising Out of SLP(C) Nos. 7710-7711/2015)
</p><p>(Non-Reportable)
</p><p>1. Heard the learned counsel for the parties.
</p><p>2. Leave granted.
</p><p>3. Upon perusal of the impugned judgment, it appears that the possession of the land in question was with the owners of the land in question in 2002-2003. Whereas, as per the respondents the possession was taken over in the year 1995-1996. There appears to be some doubt, which was not examined by the High Court.
</p><p>4. Therefore, we set aside the impugned orders and remand the matters to the High Court so that it can be decided afresh after hearing the parties.
</p><p>5. The appeals are disposed of as allowed with no order as to costs.
</p><p>Appeals disposed of
</p></div>
