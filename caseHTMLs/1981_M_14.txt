<div><h3>Mainuddin v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>6 March 1981
</b><p>
</p><p>Cr.A. No. 239 of 1981.
</p><p>The Order of the Court was as follows:
</p><p>Heard counsel. Special leave granted.
</p><p>1.  Following the order dated January 15, 1981, <a href="/case/<a href="/case/1981 Indlaw SC 4">1981 Indlaw SC 4</a>51"><a href="/case/1981 Indlaw SC 4">1981 Indlaw SC 4</a>51</a><a href="/case/<a href="/case/1981 Indlaw SC 4">1981 Indlaw SC 4</a>51"><a href="/case/1981 Indlaw SC 4">1981 Indlaw SC 4</a>51</a> passed by Justice R.S. Sarkaria and one of us, namely Justice E.S. Venkataramiah, the offences disclosed against the appellant must be held to be u/s. 324 read with S. 325 of the Penal Code.
</p><p>2.  These offences are compoundable with the permission of the Court. Since permission has been granted to the other, accused to compound the offences, we grant permission to the parties herein to compound the offence. As a consequence, the appeal will stand allowed and the appellant will stand acquitted of the charges mentioned above.
</p><p>3.  The appellant shall be released forthwith, insofar as the instant case goes.
</p><p>Appeal allowed.
</p></div>
