<div><h3>Chiranji Lal v State of Uttar Pradesh
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>24 February 1981
</b><p>
</p><p>Cr.A. No. 196 of 1981
</p><p>The order of the court was as follows:
</p><p>1.  In this appeal the complainants have filed application for permission to compound the offence. The complainants also have appeared before us personally and have stated that they have compromised the matter and have received compensation and no rancour is now left in their hearts against the appellant. Counsel for the State also has no objection to the offence being compounded.
</p><p>We therefore allow the application and sanction the compounding of the offence u/s. 320 of the Code of Criminal Procedure.
</p><p>2.  The appeal is accordingly allowed and the accused acquitted of the charges framed against him.
</p><p>Appeal allowed.
</p></div>
