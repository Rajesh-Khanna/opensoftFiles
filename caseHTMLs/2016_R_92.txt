<div><h3>Delhi Development Authority v Ran Singh and others
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>8 December 2016
</b><p>The Judgment was delivered by :<i> Kurian Joseph, J.
</i></p><p>
</p><p>Civil Appeal No. 12114 of 2016 with C. A. No. 12111/2016 @ SLP(C) No. 10012/2016, C. A. No. 12110/2016 @ SLP(C) No. 11963/2016, C. A. No. 12137/2016 @ SLP(C) No. 36721/2016 @ CC No. 5440/2016
</p><p>(Non-Reportable)
</p><p>1. Delay condoned.
</p><p>2. Leave granted.
</p><p>3. The issue, in principle, is covered against the appellant by judgments in Civil Appeal No. 8477 of 2016 arising out of Special Leave Petition (C) No. 8467 of 2015 and Civil Appeal No. 5811 of 2015 arising out of Special Leave Petition (C) No. 21545 of 2015.
</p><p>4. These appeals are, accordingly, dismissed.
</p><p>5. In the peculiar facts and circumstances of this case, the appellant is given a period of one year to exercise its liberty granted under Section 24(2) of the <a href="/acts/Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013">Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013</a> for initiation of the acquisition proceedings afresh.
</p><p>6. We make it clear that in case no fresh acquisition proceedings are initiated within the said period of one year from today by issuing a Notification under Section 11 of the Act, the appellant, if in possession, shall return the physical possession of the land to the owner.
</p><p>7. Pending applications, if any, stand disposed of.
</p><p>8. No costs.
</p><p>Appeals dismissed
