<div><h3>N. K. Nair and another v Kavanugal Aanattu Radhika
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>6  May  2005
</b><p>
</p><p>Transfer Petition (C) No. 166 of 2003
</p><p>The Order of the Court was as follows:
</p><p>1.  The prayer for transfer is refused, in view of the statement made in the counter-affidavit that the suit is at its final stage and the hearing may be finally concluded on one or two dates of hearing. However, vide para 5 of the counter-affidavit it is also stated that subsequent to transfer of the suit from the original side of the Delhi High Court to the District Court, the file itself is not traceable.
</p><p>2.  The Registrar General of the Delhi High Court shall hold an inquiry into the loss of records. Either the file may be traced out or else the record shall be reconstructed as per law and practice of the High Court in this regard. Once the case is ready for trial, it shall be so set down.
</p><p>The trial court shall appoint a clear date for holding the trial and on that date the evidence shall be recorded so that the parties are not required to come again and again. After the evidence has been completed, the hearing shall be expeditiously concluded and judgment pronounced.
</p><p>The transfer petition is dismissed.
</p><p>Petition dismissed.
</p></div>
