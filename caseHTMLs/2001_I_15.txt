<div><h3>Hira Lal v Inder Mohan Mehta and Others
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>27 August 2001
</b><p>
</p><p>Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.5805/2001
</p><p>The order of the court was as follows:
</p><p>Heard learned counsel for the parties.
</p><p>1.  We are not satisfied that the order of eviction passed by the Rent Controller which has been confirmed by the Appellate Tribunal and also the High Court in the judgment /order under challenge, warrants interference.
</p><p>2. Then Mr. Jayant Das, learned senior counsel appearing for the petitioner prayed for reasonable time to vacate the premises. Considering the said submission the petitioner is granted time till the end of February, 2002 to give vacant possession of the premises in question to the landlord-respondent, subject to his filing an undertaking on usual terms in this Court within four weeks.
</p><p>Subject to the above order the special leave petition is dismissed.
</p><p>Petition Dismissed.
