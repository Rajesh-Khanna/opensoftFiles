<div><h3>Ispat Finance Limited v Vikram C. Shah and Another
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>7 November 2000
</b><p>
</p><p>Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.13235/1999
</p><p>The Order of the Court was as follows:
</p><p>Leave granted.
</p><p>1.  Both the respondents have been served. But they have not appeared either in person or through counsel.
</p><p>2. In view of the judgment of this Court in Patheja Bros. Forging Stamping and Anr Vs. ICICI Ltd. Ors.
</p><p>proceedings against guarantors are also governed by the provisions of the <a href="/acts/Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985">Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985</a>.
</p><p>3.  However, learned counsel for the appellant seeks modification of the order of the High Court to the extent of permitting the appellant to move the BIFR for appropriate orders for the purpose of enabling the appellant to proceed against the guarantors.
</p><p>4. It will, therefore obvious that the appellant can move the BIFR for appropriate orders.
</p><p>5.  In case the BIFR grants permission to the appellant to proceed against the guarantors, it will be ...2 :2: open to the appellant to proceed with the Summary Suit, despite the order of the High Court.
</p><p>6.  We, therefore, modify the order of the High Court, to the extent indicated above. The appeal is disposed of accordingly.
</p><p>7.  We should not be understood as having said anything on the merits of the case.
</p><p>8.  The appeal is disposed of accordingly. There will be no order as to costs.
</p><p>Appeal disposed of.
</p></div>
