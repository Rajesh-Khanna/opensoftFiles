<div><h3>B. V. Star v Commissioner of Customs
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>23 September 2005
</b><p>
</p><p>C.A. No. 5885 of 2005 (D No. 9071 of 2005)
</p><p>The Order of the Court was as follows:
</p><p>Permission to file civil appeal is granted.
</p><p>1.  While considering Application No. C/S/402 of 2005 in Appeal No. C/164 of 2005 the Customs, Excise and Service Tax Appellate Tribunal, Mumbai has not only disposed of the stay application but has also disposed of the main appeal.
</p><p>2. While disposing of the main appeal liberty has been given to the parties to seek clarification from this Court with regard to liability under the judgment of this Court in the case of Commr. of Customs v. B.V. Jewels. <a href="/case/<a href="/case/2004 Indlaw SC 8">2004 Indlaw SC 8</a>4"><a href="/case/2004 Indlaw SC 8">2004 Indlaw SC 8</a>4</a>8 This liberty has been granted on the basis of some submissions made by counsel for the Revenue, which is recorded in the impugned order.
</p><p>3.  In our view, the Tribunal must decide the appeal on its own merits. No clarification is required of our judgment which speaks for itself. If the Tribunal reads the judgment it will immediately understand what has been held by this Court.
</p><p>4.  We, therefore, set aside that portion of the impugned order whereby the appeal stands disposed of. We restore the appeal to the file of the Tribunal. The Tribunal to dispose of the appeal on its own merits as expeditiously as possible and in any event within two months from today.
</p><p>5.  In the meantime, the attachment would continue but the diamonds must not be sold.
</p><p>The appeal stands disposed of accordingly.
</p><p>Appeal disposed of.
</p></div>
