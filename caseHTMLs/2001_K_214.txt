<div><h3>Kripa Shanker and Others v Jai Prakash Agarwal
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>14 September 2001
</b><p>
</p><p>Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal(Civil).........CC 5996/2001
</p><p>The Order of the Court was as follows:
</p><p>1.  The impugned order of the High Court dated 10th April, 2001 has been passed without affording any opportunity of hearing to the petitioners before us who were respondents in the High Court.
</p><p>2. The appropriate remedy available to the petitioners is to move the High Court for recalling the impugned order and deciding the landlord's application afresh after affording an opportunity of hearing to the tenants-petitioners.
</p><p>3.  The SLP is dismissed as withdrawn with liberty to the petitioners to approach the High Court. If the petitioners move the High Court within a period of two weeks from today, the bar of limitation shall not come in the way of the petitioners in filing the appropriate application before the High Court.
</p><p>Petition dismissed.
</p></div>
