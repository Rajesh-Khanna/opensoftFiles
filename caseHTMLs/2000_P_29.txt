<div><h3>State of Gujarat v P.O.A., Niruben Dineshbhai U.P.D. Patel
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>4 December 2000
</b><p>
</p><p>Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.13604/2000
</p><p>The Order of the Court was as follows:
</p><p>1.  As this is only an interlocutory order made during the pendency of the civil suit we do not think it necessary to interfere except passing certain directions.
</p><p>2.  Respondent is injucted from carrying out any further construction (except plastering, painting and such other interior work).
</p><p>If respondent wants to induct any person in possession of the building the inductee shall file an affidavit in the trial court that they will be bound by the final decree passed by the court and will not raise any other contention or right in respect of the building.
</p><p>With these directions this appeal is disposed of.
</p><p>Appeal disposed of.
</p></div>
