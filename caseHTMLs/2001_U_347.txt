<div><h3>United India Insurance Company Limited v Patricia Jean Mahajan and Others
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>7 December 2001
</b><p>
</p><p>Petition(s) for Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) No.20874/2001
</p><p>The Order of the Court was as follows:
</p><p>Heard the learned Attorney General for the petitioners.
</p><p>Issue notice to the respondents.
</p><p>1.  Shri Ashok K. Mahajan, learned counsel, who has appeared on caveat for respondents 1 to 6 accepts notice for the said respondents.
</p><p>2. No further step be taken in respect of respondents 1 to 6. Steps for service of notice on the remaining respondents be taken within six weeks.
</p><p>3.  As an interim measure, the petitioner is directed to deposit a sum of Rupees Five Crores with the Tribunal within two months from today. On the amount being deposited, the same shall be kept in a fixed deposit in a nationalised Bank for a term of six months, renewable for the similar period after its expiry.
</p><p>Order accordingly.
</p></div>
