<div><h3>Commissioner of Income Tax v Stock Exchange, Ahmedabad
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>16 December 1999
</b><p>The Judgment was delivered by :<i>The Judgment was delivered by: D P MOHAPATRA, J.
</i></p><p>
</p><p>Special Leave to Appeal (Civil) Nos. CC 6668 to 6672 of 1999
</p><p>BY THE COURT.
</p><p>Delay condoned.
</p><p>1.  As these special leave petitions are directed against an ex parte ad interim order of stay it is always open to the petitioners to move the High Court for considering the question whether the stay should be confirmed or not.
</p><p>2.  We express no opinion on the merits of the controversy between the parties and relegate the petitioners to the remedy of moving the High Court, if so advised and may pursue the same at the earliest.
</p><p>3. If any application is moved by the petitioners for considering the question of vacating the interim stay we request the High Court to consider the same at its earliest convenience and preferably within four weeks from the date of filing of such an application.
</p><p>The special leave petitions are dismissed.
</p><p>Petitions dismissed.
</p></div>
