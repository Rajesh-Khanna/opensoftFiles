<div><h3>Kashi Nath v Rajendra Prakash and Others
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>8 November 1982
</b><p>
</p><p>C.A. No. 3501 of 1982 (From the Judgment and Order Dt. 10 April 1980 of the High Court of Allahabad in Second Appeal No. 342 of 1973).
</p><p>The Order of the Court is as follows:
</p><p>Special leave is granted.
</p><p>The High Court held that the suit was bad for want of valid permission to sue Smt. Murti Devi. The appellant submits in this Court that he does not want any relief against Smt. Murti Devi and that he is prepared to give her up. In view of the statement of the appellant, the appeal is allowed and the case is remitted to the High Court for fresh consideration on merits in the light of the statement of the appellant giving up relief against respondent 4.
</p><p>1. We are not to be understood as having said anything on the merits of the case. The appeal is allowed accordingly. The appellant will pay costs of Rs 1000 to respondent 1.
</p><p>Appeal allowed.
