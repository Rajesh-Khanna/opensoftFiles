<div><h3>W.B.Freedom Fighters Organisation v U.O.I. and Others
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>12 October 2001
</b><p>
</p><p>The Order of the court was as follows:
</p><p>1.  Pursuant to the directions contained in the judgment of the Calcutta High Court reported in AIR 1995 Calcutta, 376, a State Advisory Committee was required to be formed to consider the verification report as also cases of the applicants for grant of Freedom Fighters pension. The Government of India through the Secretary, Ministry of Home Affairs vide communication dated 18.12.1997 also requested the State of West Bengal to intimate about the constitution of the Advisory Committee as well as its composition.
</p><p>2.  Learned counsel appearing for the State of West Bengal submits that he is not in a position to state whether the Advisory Committee has been constituted or not but he submits that in case the Advisory Committee has not been constituted so far, then one shall be constituted in accordance with the judgment of Calcutta High Court and communication of Government of India dated 18.12.1997.
</p><p>3. In case, an Advisory Committee is already in existence, that Committee shall examine the verification reports pertaining to the applicants and in case the Committee has so far not been constituted, the same shall be constituted within three months and cases of all the applicants referred to it for its opinion. Result of the opinion of the Advisory Committee shall be communicated to this Court on the next date.
</p><p>We adjourn the matter by four months.
</p><p>Order accordingly.
</p></div>
