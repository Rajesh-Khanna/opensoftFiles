<div><h3>Pooran Singh v State of Uttar Pradesh
</h3><p>Supreme Court of India
</p><b>8 February 1980
</b><p>The Judgment was delivered by :<i>The Judgment was delivered by: SARKARIA, J.
</i></p><p>
</p><p>Cr.A. No. 212 of 1947.
</p><p>1.   We have heard Mr. Garg, learned counsel for the appellant and Mr. O. P. Rana, learned counsel for the respondent State.
</p><p>2. Taking into consideration all the circumstances of the case, including the fact that at the time of occurrence the appellant was a raw youth of 19 years, we, while maintaining the conviction of the appellant u/s. 304 Part I, <a href="/acts/Indian Penal Code, 1860">Indian Penal Code, 1860</a>, reduce his sentence to imprisonment already undergone, which is stated to be about 18 1/2 months. Subject to this reduction in the sentence, the appeal is dismissed. The bail bond of the appellant is discharged.
</p><p>Appeal dismissed.
</p></div>
